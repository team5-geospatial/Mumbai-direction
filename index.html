INTRODUCTION

The analysis reveals significant trends in urban land dynamics, highlighting the importance of geometric assessments in urban planning. Mumbai's urban expansion reveals explosive northward growth (surge to 500 units by 2020), with significant eastern development, while western and southern sectors show constrained growth. This dramatic northern expansion reflects suburban sprawl into Thane and Navi Mumbai, driven by infrastructure projects like highways and airports, while geographic constraints (Arabian Sea/Western Ghats) and dense urban cores limit southern expansion. The pattern demands urgent infrastructure upgrades in northern growth corridors and sustainable planning for coastal eastern zones. (Units likely represent built-up area in hectares or % change.)
